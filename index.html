Start with the problem

Your current discomfort gives us clues about what needs to change.

A sleep consultation looks at patterns, not just firmness. The goal is to identify where support, cushioning, temperature, movement, or positioning may be falling short.

Lower back

Support without excessive pressure

Look for stable support beneath the waist and hips, with enough contouring to avoid creating new pressure points.

Shoulders & hips

Deeper pressure relief

Side sleepers may need more cushioning and a surface that allows the shoulder and hip to settle naturally.

Sleeping hot

Temperature-aware materials

Breathable covers, responsive foams, coils, and airflow can help create a less heat-retentive sleep environment.

Partner movement

Motion separation

Foam layers, pocketed coils, and split adjustable options can reduce how much one sleeper feels the other move.

Snoring & reflux

Upper-body elevation

Raising the head may improve comfort for some sleepers, while persistent symptoms should be discussed with a healthcare professional.

Mobility

Easier entry and exit

Adjustable height and head elevation can make transitions in and out of bed feel more manageable.

Common questions

What most shoppers need to know before choosing.

Does a firm mattress always provide better back support?

No. Firmness describes feel, while support describes how well the mattress keeps the body aligned. A mattress can feel firm and still fail to support the curves of a particular sleeper.

How do I know whether I need memory foam or a hybrid?

Memory foam often emphasizes contouring and motion isolation. Hybrids combine comfort materials with coils for added responsiveness, airflow, and edge support. The best choice depends on your body, position, and feel preference.

Can an adjustable base be used with any mattress?

Most modern foam and hybrid mattresses are compatible, but the mattress must be designed to flex safely. Always verify compatibility and warranty requirements.

What should couples consider?

Motion control, temperature, edge support, mattress size, and different comfort preferences matter. Split adjustable bases can also let each person choose a different position.
